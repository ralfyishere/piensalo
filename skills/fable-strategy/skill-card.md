# Skill card: fable-strategy

## What it does
Turns messy decisions into compared options with scenario analysis, worst-case checks, and a calibrated recommendation. Output is a decision memo: objective hierarchy, causal model with argued bottleneck, scenario-weighted EV table with reversibility, the cheapest discriminating test with pre-registered kill criteria, and labeled assumptions.

## Trigger
'recommend', 'should we', 'what's the right move' — pricing/roadmap/build-vs-buy calls, responses to competitor moves, and any decision under uncertainty with real downside.

## Counterindications
- Decisions with a known correct answer or no real downside — the scenario machinery adds latency on trivial calls.
- Scenario probabilities are judgments; the program forces them explicit but cannot make them correct. Do not read the EV table as measurement.
- No spreadsheet/calculation tool is required, but without one the EV comparison must show its arithmetic inline.

## Negative-transfer risk
Applied to small reversible choices, the full memo format (hierarchy, causal graph, scenario table) is overhead that delays an answer a one-line tradeoff would have settled. Forcing 8-15 candidates on a two-option question manufactures filler options that dilute the real comparison.
