# Skill card: fable-write

## What it does
Produces prose where every factual claim is ledgered and checked, constraints are enforced by checklist, and compression is meaning-safe. Output is a finished piece that opens with its point, fits the constraint ledger exactly, and ships with the critique log and structural alternatives considered.

## Trigger
'draft', 'write the announcement/memo/notice', 'tighten this' — high-stakes prose (announcements, notices, abstracts, memos, docs) where every claim must be defensible and omissions are costly.

## Counterindications
- Casual or low-stakes prose (a chat reply, an informal note) — the ledger and multi-pass machinery adds latency without adding safety.
- Voice/style matching depends on exemplars being provided; without them the program checks claims, not brand tone, and should say so.
- Not for fiction or exploratory freewriting, where a claim ledger is the wrong instrument.

## Negative-transfer risk
Applied to throwaway text, the draft-divergence and four-pass critique cycle turns a 2-minute reply into a 20-minute production, and the constraint-ledger formality can flatten voice where personality was the point. The compression pass, run without the ledger re-check, is exactly the meaning-mutation risk it exists to prevent.
