## Creator biography — ~120 words
Rafael “Ralph” Peña builds open-source infrastructure for AI cognition,
verification, and security. His flagship project, PIÉNSALO, is the open
artificial cortex for AI: a model-independent cognitive operating system
that helps models and agents recover the real objective, structure
difficult work, inspect their own output, apply the smallest justified
repair, verify results, and — distinctively — abstain when intervention
would cause harm. The project grew out of controlled experiments on
transferring strong model behavior, and it publishes its full evidence
map, including negative results, because failed mechanisms are as
instructive as successful ones. He also maintains agent-zero-trust, a
zero-trust scanner for repositories AI agents are about to enter, and
rulebench, a planted-trap evaluation CLI. His work centers on one
principle: autonomy requires stronger controls, not fewer.
