# Near-miss example — skill must NOT fire

**Task:** "For the monthly board deck, list last quarter's headline metrics: revenue, churn, and NPS, exactly as reported in the finance dashboard."

**Why it must not fire:** This matches a counterindication: the metrics are descriptive reporting — no fix, kill, ship, or prioritization decision hangs on them in this task, and the user explicitly wants the numbers as reported.

**Why firing would hurt:** Injecting artifact hypotheses and instrument probes into a transcription task would delay a routine deliverable and imply the reported figures are suspect without any decision at stake — pure noise for the reader.
