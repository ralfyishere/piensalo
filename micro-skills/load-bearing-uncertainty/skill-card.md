# load-bearing-uncertainty — skill card

## What it does
Before a conclusion ships, isolates the small set of uncertain assumptions the conclusion actually rests on — the ones that flip the answer if wrong — states them explicitly alongside the conclusion, and attaches the cheapest check to each. Decorative assumptions (those the conclusion survives) are deliberately left out.

## Trigger
- A recommendation, verdict, or go/no-go conclusion someone will act on is being produced.
- At least one input to that conclusion was assumed, remembered, or quoted rather than observed or recomputed this session.
- The draft hedges inside the conclusion ("probably", "should be", "assuming...") without isolating which assumption bears the load.

## Counterindications
- Every input was verified live this session — there is nothing to isolate.
- No conclusion is being offered yet (exploratory brainstorming, option listing).
- The conclusion has been checked to be insensitive to every assumption.

## Negative-transfer risk
Hedging overload: if the skill fires on exploratory work, or surfaces every assumption instead of only the flip set, the answer reads as evasive and the real load-bearing signal drowns. Overall distraction risk is low — the procedure is capped at ~250 added tokens and step 5 explicitly suppresses decorative assumptions.

## Evidence level
DESIGNED — specified from documented reasoning-failure modes; not yet executed as a packaged skill.
