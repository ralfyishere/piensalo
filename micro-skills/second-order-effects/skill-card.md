# second-order-effects — skill card

## What it does
Before a change with dependents is endorsed, extends the impact analysis exactly one level past the direct effects: for each first-order outcome, names who or what adapts to it (users, teams, scripts, markets, attackers, the metric itself) and what that adaptation breaks or shifts — then orders the rollout so that a wrong projection fails in the cheaper direction. Output is a two-level effect list plus one named harm with mitigation, or an explicit none-found-after-search.

## Trigger
- A change with adapting dependents is proposed: rollout, migration, deprecation, policy, incentive, pricing, quota, reorg, mandate, or a default-change affecting everyone.
- The draft's impact analysis stops at direct effects — no adaptation traced.

## Counterindications
- The change is verified (not presumed) to have no dependents or adapting agents — fully local.
- Second-order analysis for this same change already exists this session.
- Pure information requests with no change proposed.

## Negative-transfer risk
Speculative doom-chains: stacking third-order fears until nothing ships, or bolting adaptation analysis onto trivially local edits. Distraction risk is medium — this skill adds real analysis weight — so the procedure is hard-capped at two levels, every consequence must name its mechanism, and the token ceiling is ~300.
