# rederive-the-numbers — skill card

## What it does
Partitions every number in a draft into quoted (traceable verbatim to a source) or derived (computed from other numbers), then independently recomputes each load-bearing derived figure from its stated inputs — by a different route than the one that produced it, using actual code for anything beyond trivial arithmetic. Mismatches stop the line: the divergence is located and resolved, never averaged away.

## Trigger
- Percentages, multiples, or ratios appear that are typically derived rather than quoted.
- Monetary figures appear that are usually computed (unit price × volume, projections, cost estimates).
- Aggregates are named — totals, sums, averages, medians, per-user/per-month rates, growth, deltas — which are derived by definition.
- The draft asserts a computed number with no visible recomputation.

## Counterindications
- Every figure is a labeled quotation from a source; nothing was derived.
- Numbers are illustrative only; no claim or decision depends on them.
- The same figure was already recomputed this session and the result recorded.

## Negative-transfer risk
Recomputing throwaway figures nobody acts on — burning tokens and attention auditing illustrative numbers. The load-bearing filter is the mitigation: only figures a decision or claim rests on get the treatment, within a ~200-token ceiling. Distraction risk is low.

## Evidence level
EXPERIMENTALLY_TESTED — repair content of this kind lifted a weak model on procedural tasks in controlled runs; the effect is task-concentrated, and automatic router selection of micro-skills is a separate, unproven layer.
