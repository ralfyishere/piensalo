# Positive example — long report brief with buried constraints

**Task (250 words, abridged):** "Write a competitive-landscape summary for the board... cover the top five vendors... Pricing must come only from public list prices — never quote figures from analyst calls. Keep the whole document under 600 words. Do not mention the pending acquisition. Ideally, open with a one-line takeaway..."

**Trigger appears:** Long brief (>150 words) with restrictive clauses ("must ... only", "never", "under 600 words", "do not mention") scattered mid-paragraph — exactly where constraints fall out of working memory.

**Procedure applied:**
1. Constraints extracted verbatim (quoted, not paraphrased).
2. Hard/soft classified.
3. Draft checked against each hard constraint.

**Required output produced:**

| Verbatim quote | Hard/soft | Status |
|---|---|---|
| "Pricing must come only from public list prices — never quote figures from analyst calls" | hard | violated → fixed: Vendor C's price (sec. 3) was from an analyst call; replaced with the public list price |
| "Keep the whole document under 600 words" | hard | satisfied (draft: 542 words) |
| "Do not mention the pending acquisition" | hard | satisfied (full-text search: no mention) |
| "Ideally, open with a one-line takeaway" | soft | taken (line 1 is the takeaway) |

The violation was named and fixed before the deliverable was presented, and the table stays with the working notes so later edits re-check against it.
