# audience-constraint-check — skill card

## What it does
Checks a deliverable against its actual consumer: names who reads it and what they do next, lists the constraints that consumer imposes (length, format, register, confidentiality), and scopes every claim to what the consumer will safely assert downstream.

## Trigger
- The deliverable is directed at a consumer beyond the requester — an email, memo, report, briefing, handoff, or summary "to" or "for" a team, board, executive, investor, on-call engineer, client, or customer.
- The reader or actor is not the person making the request, or the text will be forwarded, announced, or acted on by someone absent from the session.
- Post-draft signal: the draft's length, format, or jargon level conflicts with the named consumer's constraints.

## Counterindications
- The requester is the sole consumer — working notes with no downstream reader.
- Audience constraints were already gathered and applied earlier in the session.
- Interactive conversation where the consumer is present and can push back.

## Negative-transfer risk
Low distraction risk. Main failure mode: over-fitting to an imagined audience and stripping technical content the real reader needed. Constraints must come from the task or known facts about the consumer, not stereotype.
