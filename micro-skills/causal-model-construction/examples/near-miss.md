# Near-miss example (must NOT fire)

**Case:** "The page title looks wrong — try changing the `<title>` tag and reload." A one-line edit, instantly reversible, verifiable in seconds.

**Why it must not fire:** the action is trivially reversible and cheaper to try than to model (counterindication). One reload settles the question faster than any written chain could.

**Why firing would hurt:** drawing a causal diagram for a near-free, instantly checkable edit burns tokens and time on theorizing when direct trial strictly dominates — the modeling cost exceeds the entire cost of just doing it and looking.
