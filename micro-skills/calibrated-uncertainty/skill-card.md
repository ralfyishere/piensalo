# calibrated-uncertainty — skill card

## What it does
Tags every load-bearing claim with its actual evidence level (verified / inferred / assumed / guessed), strips certainty language from anything unverified, and attaches numeric credences only where a date and an observable could later score them. Keeps verified facts plainly asserted so the genuine uncertainty stays visible.

## Trigger
- The user asks for likelihood or confidence: "how confident/likely/sure", probability, odds, forecast, prediction, estimate, "will this work/scale/hold".
- The response makes claims about unobserved, future, or unverified state — including vendor/library behavior recalled from memory.
- Post-draft signals: certainty words ("definitely", "guaranteed", "100%", "can't fail") attached to claims, or load-bearing claims about unverified state carrying no evidence-level distinction.

## Counterindications
- Every claim in the response was verified this session — hedging observed facts miscalibrates downward.
- Pure preference/taste questions with no factual referent to calibrate about.

## Negative-transfer risk
Low distraction risk. Main failure mode: uniform hedging that buries the signal — every sentence qualified until the reader can't locate the real uncertainty. The contrast rule (verified facts stay plainly asserted) is the mitigation.
