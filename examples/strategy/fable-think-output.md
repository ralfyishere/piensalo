Hard requirements first, preferences second — a hard requirement failed is
a disqualification, not a deduction.

**Gate check (binding):**
- SwiftParcel — cold chain is certified only on carrier legs, **not
  end-to-end**. For chilled food this is the one non-negotiable.
  **Disqualified**, regardless of its 94/100 score.
- Veloway — 24-month exclusivity violates the no-exclusivity requirement.
  **Disqualified.** (Also the kind of clause that converts a bad first year
  into a two-year problem — low reversibility.)
- BoxHarbor — 88% 48h coverage misses the 90% floor. **Disqualified.**
- Northlane — passes all three gates.

**Decision: Northlane** at $4.85/box. It is the *only* candidate that
clears every hard requirement; among survivors, preferences don't even come
into play.

**What would change this:** if the 90% coverage floor is actually
negotiable (it's worth asking who set it and why), BoxHarbor re-enters at
$0.45/box cheaper — that question is worth one email before signing.
Risk noted: single-survivor decisions are fragile; re-run the gate check if
any vendor updates its certification status before contract.
